In this journey you will see how the User Interface evolved from the first mobile phones to modern smartphones.


1996

Palm OS

Palm OS was a mobile operating system for personal digital assistants (PDAs) that was initially developed by Palm, Inc.
It was designed for ease of use with a touchscreen-based graphical user interface (GUI) and was known for its reliability and efficiency.
Palm OS was also one of the first operating systems to include a built-in application programming interface (API), which made it easy for developers to create third-party applications for Palm devices.

1998

Symbian OS

Symbian OS was a mobile operating system (OS) that was popular in the late 1990s and early 2000s.
It was originally developed for personal digital assistants (PDAs) but was later ported to smartphones.
Symbian OS was the most popular mobile OS in the world for a time, and it was used on a wide variety of devices from a number of different manufacturers.

2005

Windows Mobile

Windows Mobile was a mobile operating system developed by Microsoft for smartphones and personal digital assistants (PDAs).
It was based on the Windows CE kernel and first appeared as the Pocket PC 2000 operating system.
Windows Mobile was designed to have features and appearance somewhat similar to desktop versions of Windows, such as a Start menu, a taskbar, and a desktop.

2007

BlackBerry OS 4.5

BlackBerry OS was a proprietary mobile operating system developed by Canadian company BlackBerry Limited for its BlackBerry line of smartphones.
It was known for its focus on productivity and security, particularly its support for push email and corporate communication tools.

2007

iPhone


and Multi-Touch

The iPhone revolution revolutionized the mobile phone industry when it first launched in 2007.
Before the iPhone, most smartphones were feature phones that were not as user-friendly or powerful as the iPhone.
The iPhone's touchscreen interface, its powerful hardware, and its vast app library made it a must-have for many consumers.

2008

Android

The launch of Android marked a significant turning point in the mobile computing landscape.
In November 2007, Google unveiled the first public beta of its open-source mobile operating system, sparking a new era of innovation and competition in the smartphone market.

2008

Symbian S60 Touch

Symbian S60 Touch was a user interface (UI) adaptation of the Symbian S60 platform for touchscreen-based smartphones.
It was developed by Nokia and released in 2008 as part of Symbian OS version 9.4.
S60 Touch introduced a number of features that were specifically designed for touchscreen interaction, such as gesture recognition, pinch-to-zoom, and a virtual keyboard.

2010

Bada

Bada was a mobile operating system developed by Samsung Electronics for smartphones and tablet computers.
The operating system was based on a hybrid kernel, which was a combination of the Linux kernel and a proprietary real-time operating system (RTOS) kernel.
This made Bada OS more efficient and responsive than other mobile operating systems at the time.

2010

Windows Phone 7

Windows Phone 7 (codenamed Photon) was the first version of the Windows Phone mobile client operating system developed by Microsoft.
It was released on October 21, 2010, and replaced Windows Mobile.
Windows Phone 7 was designed to be a more user-friendly and visually appealing operating system than its predecessor.

2011

Meego

Meego and the Nokia N9 were a remarkable pair of mobile innovations that never quite reached their full potential.
Meego was a Linux-based operating system developed by Nokia and Intel, while the Nokia N9 was a flagship smartphone that exclusively ran Meego.
Meego is also known for being the first mobile operating system to use the swipe gesture for navigation.

2013

BlackBerry 10

BlackBerry OS 10 marked a significant shift for BlackBerry, introducing a fresh new look and feel, enhanced multitasking capabilities, and a focus on multimedia and social networking.
This marked a departure from the company's traditional focus on business-oriented features like email and messaging.

2023

Android 14

Android 14 is the current major version of the Android operating system.
It was released to the public on October 4, 2023, and is available for a variety of devices, including smartphones, tablets, and foldables.
Android 14 focuses on three key areas: personalization, multi-device experiences, and protection.

2023

iOS 17

As of today, the current version of iOS is iOS 17. It was released on September 18, 2023.
iOS 17 is compatible with iPhone 15 and later.
It introduces several new features, including a new full-screen experience called StandBy, improved messaging capabilities, and enhanced privacy protections.
